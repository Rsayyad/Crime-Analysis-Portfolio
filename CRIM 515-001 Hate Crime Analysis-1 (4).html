Research Question:

This study examines the impact of the Atlanta Spa Shooting (March 2021) on hate crime trends in the United States, specifically focusing on the change in the frequency of reported hate crimes before and after the incident. The research investigates behavior (hate crime incidents), space (United States), and time (2019-2023). Key variables include the number of hate crime incidents and the bias motivation (particularly Anti-Asian), measured through statistical summaries and a t-test comparing pre- and post-shooting periods.

Research Question:

Did the Atlanta Spa Shooting in March 2021 result in a significant increase in reported hate crimes in the United States?

Literature Review:

Agathangelou and Killian (2021) explore when anti-Asian violence, such as the mass shootings of Asian women at Atlanta spas, will be seen as a serious issue. They trace the violence to U.S. imperial politics and the U.S.-China rivalry. The study uses a historical and political analysis, looking at past conflicts in Japan, Korea, and Vietnam. The authors argue that this violence reflects political strategies that control people and land. They find that Asian women are particularly vulnerable to sexualized violence due to these historical divisions and call for action to disrupt these systems.

Citation:

Agathangelou, A., & Killian, K. (2021). Violence against Asians: When is racial hate a crime? Journal of Feminist Scholarship, 18(18). https://doi.org/10.23860/jfs.2021.18.09

Data:

This study uses the FBIs Hate Crime Statistics dataset, which has 253,776 records of hate crimes from 1991 to 2023. It includes details on incidents, offenses, victims, and offenders, with law enforcement agencies reporting through the UCR Program. The dataset also provides geographic data like region, state, agency, and population. I used it to group hate crimes by year and bias type, count incidents, and calculate percentages. I used nationwide data to show the impact across the entire country. A major advantage is its large size and easy access, allowing for detailed analysis. However, it only covers data up to 2023, limiting the analysis of recent trends.

Methods:

I used R software with packages like tidyverse and data.table for data analysis and visualization. The data was cleaned by converting date columns, creating new variables like month and yearmonth, and filtering for 2019 to 2023. I calculated yearly and monthly hate crime counts and ran a T-test to compare crime rates before and after the Atlanta Spa Shooting in March 2021. I used the mean function in R to calculate the average number of hate crimes per year and applied the compare function to my table to observe the changes. I created bar plots using ggplot to show changes in hate crimes nationwide. This approach combined data cleaning, hypothesis testing, and visualization to assess the shootings impact.

Findings

Finding 1: Trends in Hate Crimes from 2019 to 2023

To examine the impact of the Atlanta Spa Shooting on hate crime trends, I analyzed hate crime data from 2019 to 2023. The data reveals a steady rise in the number of hate crimes over the five-year period. As illustrated in Visual 1, hate crimes increased from 7,869 reported incidents in 2019 (15% of the total) to 9,958 in 2020 (18.98%). Following the Atlanta Spa Shooting, the most significant spike occurred in 2021, with incidents rising to 11,014 (20.99%). This upward rise continued in 2022 and 2023, with 11,766 (22.43%) and 11,858 (22.60%) reported cases. The figure visually emphasizes the consistent rise in hate crimes, particularly in the years following the shooting, suggesting a long-term trend of increasing incidents.

Finding 2: Hate Crimes Compared to the Five-Year Average

To assess changes in hate crime patterns, I compared yearly totals to the five-year average of 10,493 incidents. In 2019 and 2020, reported hate crimes were below the average by 2,624 and 535 cases. In 2021, the year of the Atlanta Spa Shooting, the total exceeded the average by 521 cases. This increase continued in 2022 and 2023, with incidents rising above the average by 1,273 and 1,365 cases. Hate crimes remained consistently higher than the average after 2021, indicating a rise in reported incidents.

Finding 3: Nationwide Rise in Anti-Asian Hate Crimes After the Atlanta Spa Shooting

To analyze the impact of the Atlanta Spa Shooting, I examined nationwide trends in anti-Asian hate crimes from 2019 to 2023. As seen in Visual 2, the data shows a sharp increase in 2021, with incidents peaking at 758, compared to just 38 in 2019 and 334 in 2020. This spike aligns with the aftermath of the shooting, reflecting heightened anti-Asian sentiment across the country. Although the numbers slightly declined in 2022 (508 incidents) and 2023 (407 incidents), they remained significantly higher than pre-2021 levels. The findings suggest that anti-Asian hate crimes surged nationwide following the shooting and continued at an elevated rate in the following years.

Finding 4: Significant Increase in Hate Crimes Per Month After the Atlanta Spa Shooting

To measure the statistical significance of hate crime trends before and after the Atlanta Spa Shooting, I conducted a t-test comparing the average number of hate crime incidents per month nationwide. As seen in Visual 3, the average monthly incidents increased from 738 pre-shooting to 980.5 post-shooting. The t-test result (t = -5.16, p < 0.0001) confirms that this increase is statistically significant, indicating that the rise in hate crimes after the shooting was not due to random variation.

Finding 5: Impact of the Atlanta Spa Shooting on Hate Crime Trends Overall

The data from Visual 1 and Visual 2 highlight the significant impact of the Atlanta Spa Shooting on hate crimes from 2019 to 2023. Visual 1 shows a steady rise in overall hate crimes, from 7,869 in 2019 to 11,858 in 2023, with a sharp spike to 11,014 in 2021. Visual 2 reveals a dramatic increase in anti-Asian hate crimes, rising from 38 in 2019 to 758 in 2021. Although anti-Asian incidents dropped to 508 in 2022 and 407 in 2023, they remained much higher than before 2021. These trends confirm that the shooting led to a lasting rise in both overall and anti-Asian hate crimes.

So What:

This study is important for criminal justice professionals because it shows how major events impact hate crime trends. Using RStudio, I analyzed data and found a rise in overall hate crimes after the Atlanta Spa Shooting, with a sharp increase in anti-Asian incidents. These findings can help improve hate crime prevention, law enforcement training, and community outreach. A successful application would include better reporting systems, targeted patrols, and public awareness campaigns. Police can use data-driven approaches to track hate crimes and focus resources on high-risk areas. Further research could focus on identifying geographic hot-spots where hate crimes are most frequent. Mapping these areas would allow police to deploy resources more strategically and provide better protection for vulnerable communities.
